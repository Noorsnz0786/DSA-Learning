# Mission: Data Structures & Algorithms for the Frappe Interview

## Why
The user cracked the first round of a Frappe (Python) developer interview. The next
round is DSA. They want to walk in able to solve common interview problems AND clearly
explain their thinking out loud — because this round is mostly verbal: the interviewer
gives problems and judges *how* the candidate reasons, not just the final answer.

**Updated after a real interview attempt (2026-06-26):** The user sat a DSA interview and
could not solve a single problem. Root cause they identified themselves: **no concept
clarity and no way to DECIDE which technique to use, when, and why.** They have never
studied DSA before. So the real, primary need is the *decision-making / pattern-recognition
layer* — taught from absolute zero, in plain language, with real-life examples, going
deep step by step — BEFORE problem drilling. If they can look at a new problem and
confidently pick the right tool (and say why), the solving follows.

## Success looks like
- Confidently solve ~50 of the most common interview problems in Python.
- For any solution, state its **time and space complexity** and *why*.
- Explain *why* a data structure / technique fits a problem, and *when* to reach for it.
- Think out loud fluently: restate problem → brute force → optimize → code → test.
- Recognise the standard **patterns** (two pointers, hashing, sliding window, BFS/DFS, etc.)
  fast enough to apply them under interview pressure.

## Constraints
- Timeline: **2–4 weeks**.
- Coding level: "okay-ish" — can write basic Python, gets stuck on logic-heavy problems.
- Language: **Python**.
- Format: mostly **verbal / live** — problems given on the spot, thinking assessed.
- Learning style: minimal theory, real-life analogies, interactive animations, learn-by-doing.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Hard competitive-programming topics (segment trees, advanced DP, flows, etc.).
- Language-specific trivia unrelated to problem solving.
- Frappe/ERPNext framework knowledge (covered in earlier rounds).

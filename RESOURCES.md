# DSA for Interviews — Resources

## Knowledge

- [NeetCode — Roadmap & Practice](https://neetcode.io/roadmap)
  The single best free interview-DSA resource. A dependency-ordered roadmap of topics,
  each with a curated problem list and free video walkthroughs. Use for: what to study
  next, and watching a clear explanation after you attempt a problem.
- [freeCodeCamp — Big O Cheat Sheet & Time Complexity Chart](https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/big-o-cheat-sheet-time-complexity-chart/)
  Clean reference for Big-O of common operations and data structures. Use for: looking up
  "what's the complexity of X" while practising.
- [GeeksforGeeks — Complete DSA Roadmap](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/dsa/complete-roadmap-to-learn-dsa-from-scratch/)
  Encyclopedic explanations + practice for every topic. Use for: a second explanation when
  one source doesn't click, and topic-wise practice problems.
- [LeetCode](https://leetcode.com/problemset/) (free tier)
  Where the 50 problems live. Use for: actually solving + the discussion tab after.
- [VisuAlgo](https://visualgo.net/en)
  Animated visualisations of data structures and algorithms. Use for: *seeing* how a
  structure changes step by step (sorting, trees, graphs).

- [Blind 75 — the canonical interview problem list (NeetCode)](https://neetcode.io/practice/practice/blind75)
  The 75 problems that cover every interview pattern. Use for: a focused problem set if you want more than the course's ~70.
- [Preplaced — 50 Most-Asked DSA Interview Questions (2025)](https://www.preplaced.in/blog/50-most-asked-dsa-interview-questions)
  Topic-wise list of frequently-asked questions. Used to shape the Mock Drill (Section 12).
- [InterviewBit — Data Structure Interview Questions](https://www.interviewbit.com/data-structure-interview-questions/)
  Good for the *verbal/theory* questions (definitions, trade-offs, complexities) — what an interviewer asks out loud.

## Wisdom (Communities)

- [r/leetcode](https://www.reddit.com/r/leetcode/)
  Active community for interview prep, problem strategy, and "is this normal" reassurance.
  Use for: pattern advice, mock-interview partners, decompressing interview anxiety.
- [NeetCode Discord](https://neetcode.io/) (link on site)
  Friendly, focused on interview prep. Use for: getting unstuck on a specific problem.

## Gaps
- No Frappe-specific DSA round write-ups found. Frappe's process is practical (take-home +
  live coding), so prep targets *general* problem-solving thinking, not exotic algorithms.
